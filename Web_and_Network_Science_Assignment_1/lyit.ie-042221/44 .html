FAQs - Non-EU Students

1. I am a Non-EU student, how do I apply?

All Non-EU students apply to LYIT’s International Office for admissions to LYIT (do not apply through CAO).

2. I am Irish but completed my Secondary School in another country, how do I apply?

Irish nationals that have completed Secondary School exams outside of the state need to apply through the International dept. If you are unsure, please contact us.

3. How much does it cost to study at LYIT?

Standard Non-EU fees are €10,000 for a Masters Programme and €10,000 per annum, for an Undergraduate programme.

4. Where is LYIT located?

LYIT is in a town called Letterkenny (population of 20,000 people) in the County of Donegal.

5. Is Letterkenny in the Republic of Ireland or in Northern Ireland?

Letterkenny is in the Republic of Ireland but is near the border with Northern Ireland.

6. Can I stay in Ireland after I finish my degree?

If you graduate with a Hons Degree (this is called Level 8 in Ireland) you are eligible for 12 months stay back. If you graduate with a Masters Postgraduate Degree, you are eligible for up to 24 months stay back.

7. What is the academic requirement to apply for an Undergraduate Degree at LYIT?

You need to have completed and passed Secondary School (High School) to do an Undergraduate Degree in LYIT.

8. Is LYIT in the Interim List of Eligible Programmes (ILEP)?

Our Undergraduate and Postgraduate programmes are on the International Register.

9. Is LYIT a private Institute?

LYIT is a Government funded Higher Education Institute.

10. Who issues the awards of students who complete their degree in LYIT?

LYIT awards its own Degrees and the Graduation ceremony is held in October each year.

11. English is not my first language, what are the English requirements to apply for a course?

If you are not a native English speaker, you will need IELTS 5.5 for Undergraduate Programmes and 6.0 for Postgraduate Programmes.

12. If I don’t meet the English requirements, can I do an English language course at LYIT before starting my chosen Undergraduate/Masters course?

We do not run an English Language foundation programme but if you have IELTS 5.0 we will assist you to spend time at an English Language School prior to starting at LYIT. (Additional fees apply).

13. When I receive the offer letter, do I have to pay a deposit?

If you are offered a place, you will need to pay a deposit if asked to do so in the offer letter.

14. When do I have to pay my tuition fees?

Full balance of fees should be received by 1 June if you are starting in Sept.

15. How do I pay my tuition fees?

Payment of fees is made via Pay to Study. Details will be in your offer letter.

16. Do I have to buy health insurance?

You will need to take out LYIT’s online insurance prior to arrival. Non-EU students can purchase either Medicover Standard (€150) or Medicover Superior (€200). Details will be in your offer letter. You can purchase using the following link: http://studyandprotect.com/. The insurance provider is: Study And Protect, 17 Herbert Place, Dublin 2, Ireland, P: +353 1-6395800. If you have any queries in relation to this, please email: info@studyandprotect.com. They are open 9am – 5pm Monday to Friday and respond to emails sent directly to this mailbox within 1-2 working days.

Please note it is compulsory to take out this Medical Insurance prior to your start date.

17. What do I do if I need a visa to study in Ireland?

If you require a visa to study in Ireland, please check for details online for your specific country. LYIT International office will provide you with an offer letter and proof of payment to assist with your visa application. See visa and immigration section for further details.

18. Can I apply for courses of any Department?

Non-EU students are not eligible to apply for LYIT’s Nursing programmes.

19. What are the starting date of courses at LYIT?

September 2019 (date TBC)

20. Can I use an agent to do the application process for me?

If you wish to use an agent as part of the application and visa process please inform LYIT and we will let you know if the agent has an agreement with us. There should be no cost to the student to use the services of an agent.

21. Do I have to apply for a permit once I am in Ireland?

All Non-EU students have to get an Irish Residence Permit Card (IRP). These used to be called the GNIB card. You will make an appointment for your IRP card after you have registered at LYIT in Sept (or Jan). The appointment is at Letterkenny Garda Station (police station). The IRP card is to be renewed annually after registration and costs €300 per year. G1 card also costs €300 per year. The G1 is the card you receive after you have completed your studies.

22. Can I work while I am studying in Ireland?

With the IRP card, you are entitled to work 20 hours per week during term time and 40 hours per week during the holidays.

23. Does LYIT have a campus residence or accommodation?

LYIT do not have their own accommodation on campus. We will give you a list of suitable accommodation with trusted private landlords. Prices range from €250 - €380 per month for single room in a shared house with shared bathroom and kitchen. There will be some additional fees for bills such as electricity and Wi-Fi. We recommend that accommodation be booked by May if possible. LYIT International office will assist with accommodation advice if requested.

24. How do I get to Letterkenny?

Most students arrive into Dublin Airport and get the public Bus to Letterkenny, this take three and a half hours. You can purchase a bus ticket online or on the bus. There are two bus companies travelling from the Airport to Letterkenny contact the International office to ask for timetable.

25. Does LYIT provide any type of transportation from the airport?

In September, we send a Bus to the Airport to collect international students to bring them to Letterkenny. The date for this will be in your offer letter. This bus is free of charge. If you use a public bus, it will be €22.50 for an adult single ticket. Public Buses are located outside of Terminal 1 at Dublin Airport.

26. Does LYIT have international students coming only from Europe?

LYIT has International students from Europe and outside of Europe.

27. Does LYIT provide for any student services?

LYIT has great student services (Medical, Career Service, Student Union, Clubs and Societies) all of which are free to LYIT students.

28. If my visa is refused, can I get my tuition fee money back?

If you are unsuccessful in getting a visa LYIT will make a full refund less any bank charges.

29. Can I withdraw if I decide not to do the course anymore after I registered?

After registering students can withdraw and are entitled to a partial fees refund up until 31 Oct. Admissions will assist you in calculating the refund amount. No refunds permitted after 31 Oct (for Sept intake) and after 31 March (for a Jan intake).

30. Can I apply via CAO?

Students presenting non-EU Exams/Awards as a basis for entry should apply directly to the International Office and not via the CAO
