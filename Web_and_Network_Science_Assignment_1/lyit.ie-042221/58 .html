Learning Support

When you feel good about life you can embrace your studies with confidence. That is why we have such strong learning support structures in place for all of our students.

The Learning Support Unit at LYIT, titled the Curve, was established to encourage and support all students throughout their time at LYIT.

At The Curve, we offer invaluable advice and support to a diverse range of students and we operate an open-door policy at all times.

Supports Include:

Mathematics Learning Centre

The purpose of the Mathematics Learning Centre (MLC) is to support students’ mathematics learning across all courses in LYIT. Perhaps you lack confidence in your ability to do mathematics? Perhaps you missed out somewhere along the line or had a negative experience learning maths? Whatever the reason, don’t wait until it’s too late. We are here to help all students and offer the following services:

  • Small group tutorial support in Maths and Physics topics
  • Limited individual consultations
  • Exam revision support sessions
  • Online support via Blackboard and email
  • Access to special resources including digital pencasts

Please call up to Room 3460 and talk to us about how we can help you. Alternatively email our two staff members Joe (joe.english@lyit.ie) and Lucia (lucia.ramsey@lyit.ie).

It is important to understand that the MLC is not a replacement for attending lectures. Also, we do not offer a service for completing maths assignments.

Communication Learning Centre

The aim of the Communication Learning Centre (CLC) is to help students with academic writing and study skills. This service is for all students at LYIT.

The CLC provide a number of workshops through the academic year:

  • Preparing and delivering an effective presentation
  • Writing an essay: advice on process and structure
  • Advice on referencing and avoiding plagiarism
  • Advice on exam preparation and technique
  • Turnitin advice and information.

Please call up to Room 3456 and talk to us about how we can help you. Alternatively email our two staff members Deirdre (deirdre.mcclay@lyit.ie) and Karen (karen.quinn@lyit.ie).

QuickScan

QuickScan is an easy to use, multi-functional screening questionnaire that takes 10-20 minutes to complete and identifies specific areas of learning strengths and weaknesses. All first year students at LYIT undertake the Quickscan questionnaire on Registration day in September.

On completion, QuickScan generates a report that identifies:

  • The student’s preferred learning style, giving strategies and information on how to get the best out of time spent studying
  • Any learning supports that will help student learning
  • Useful studying skills
  • Maths difficulties
  • Dyspraxia
  • Visual stress
  • Learning confidence.
